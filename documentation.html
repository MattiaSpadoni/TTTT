Concept

The 1445 typographical style aims to reproduce the late italian medieval (or humanistic) manuscript.

Layout

The style starts from the use of a parchment background. The body of the HTML is set to a dark gray color, after it every article has a background image made by a parchment. It is a .png file, 721x864 px, it is repeated along the y axis to cover the all document without being streched. I cropped the image along its borders to make more realistic the shape of a parchment folio (sheet). Following a common humanistic practice I decided to use the golden ratio canon rule to designate the text area. Through the use of CSS percentage property I set the article padding to be coherent with the Golden ratio Rule. Bottom padding is 55%, the top is 34%, the left one to 21% and the right one is 13%. Through media queries I adapted the proportion to a more adequate one starting from a width minor than 768 px. I follow the Fibonacci sequence to maintain the golden ratio. The page want to reproduce the typical humanistic way of creating layout. I created 2 columns, each one represent an ideal page of the manuscript. According to the golden ratio proportion the gap between columns is made by 10% of the container, resulting in the 5% for each "page". To make complete this division, following the HTML structure, I added a division between each section in order to imitate the folio change

.

Clarification about possible doubts:

It starts from dividing in 2 columns the document on a parchment background, in order to imitate a common use in ancient manuscripts. On the other side the fact that we are online blocks us from trying to reproduce also the structure of a book divided in pages. At the same time I modeled the layout to be loyal to the page structure typical of a humanistic manuscript through the use of the golden ratio. From our historical sources I understood that the blank space around the text is bigger under and above the text, is medium on the external side of the page and lower on the internal one. Spaces are not created randomly and everyone is proportional to the others through golden ration. I maintained this proportion using a 13% top and bottom padding, a 8% left and right padding and a column gap of 10% (resulting in a gap of 5% for each column). These numbers were not random, in fact I chose them from the Fibonacci sequence. In order to avoid a displeasent appearance on small and medium screens I decided to break the 2 column pagination when going under the 768px screen definition. At the same time I maintained the golden ratio proportion among all text margins. The font is derived from the formalization of one of humanistic fonts. Paleography studies says that humanistic fonts were deeply bounded to carolingian fonts trying to recover the ancient roman way of writing. Conrad Sweynheym and Arnold Pannartz were the first humanists to introduce movable type printing press in Italy, they based their font on the Humanistic minuscole used during their time. According to this fact I chose to use their font as one of the major font during that period. One of elements that differentiate humanistic minuscole from carolingian one was the lighter hink on manuscripts. This fact is reproduced by CSS through text opacity. The use of subscriptions reproduce the lines left by monks when they used special tools to avoid writing crooked. Paragraph lines are a bit more separated one from the other in order to represent the typical humanistic manuscript. In fact besides their inspiration to carolingian way of writing, humanistic writers prefers to use a lighter page with much more white space than past manuscripts. The humanistic way of writing born from Petrarca as a reaction to the heavy and dark page of late gothic manuscripts. Indeed it is why I tried to maintain a light page with a lot of space and a weaker color of text than usual. The initial sections' capital lettere is, on the other side, not philological, this fact is linked to the lack of historically correct capital letter fonts. After an unsuccesful research I used the most correct free font I was able to find on the internet.

Art Deco

Author: Miaria Chiara Frangipane

The term "Art Deco" is taken from the name of the 1925 Paris exhibition titled Exposition Internationale des Arts Décoratifs et Industriels Modernes. The most popular and respected French artists and craftsmen of the day showcased their works. Art Deco could be addressed as a modern and dynamic interpretation of the precedent movement: Art Nouveau. Starting from the natular shapes and floral details, Art Deco embraces sinous curves and horror vacui tendencies combining them with a modern interpretation. The Machine Age transformed the rounded naturalistic motifs of Nouveau with geometric, angular and streamlined motifs, like zigzags and chevrons. Embracing the future in all its glamourousness was the focus of Art Deco.

The script

We created 2 different scripts for 2 different reasons. The first script is the one that change the content and the style of the iframe. a little Javascript is used firstly to change the SRC of the iframe and then through the onload wait until the page is loaded and then changing the CSS of the page. During the charge the iframe is hidden making impossible for users to see the changes going on.

The second script is used to automatically populate the page of the EU-directive. In fact at the beginning the page is empty and only through 2 fetch (a mechanism to handle in a simpler way calls to the server) the page is populated with all tags coming from 2 different HTML documents. We realized this script in order to have something that automatically take elements of different language versions of the same document and puts them in a grid without a long manual encoding.
