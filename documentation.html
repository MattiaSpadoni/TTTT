Humanistic manuscript (mid XV century)

Author: Mattia Spadoni

Context: esame Web Technologies, master degree in Digital Humanities and Digital Knowledge, academic year: 2018-2019

Description: the style want to reproduce the late medieval manuscript, ideally the humanistic 1440 manuscript. It starts from dividing in 2 columns the document on a parchment background, in order to imitate a common use in ancient manuscripts. On the other side the fact that we are online blocks us from trying to reproduce also the structure of a book divided in pages. At the same time I modeled the layout to be loyal to the page structure typical of a humanistic manuscript through the use of the golden ratio. From our historical sources I understood that the blank space around the text is bigger under and above the text, is medium on the external side of the page and lower on the internal one. Spaces are not created randomly and everyone is proportional to the others through golden ration. I maintained this proportion using a 13% top and bottom padding, a 8% left and right padding and a column gap of 10% (resulting in a gap of 5% for each column). These numbers were not random, in fact I chose them from the Fibonacci sequence. In order to avoid a displeasent appearance on small and medium screens I decided to break the 2 column pagination when going under the 768px screen definition. At the same time I maintained the golden ratio proportion among all text margins. The font is derived from the formalization of one of humanistic fonts. Paleography studies says that humanistic fonts were deeply bounded to carolingian fonts trying to recover the ancient roman way of writing. Conrad Sweynheym and Arnold Pannartz were the first humanists to introduce movable type printing press in Italy, they based their font on the Humanistic minuscole used during their time. According to this fact I chose to use their font as one of the major font during that period. One of elements that differentiate humanistic minuscole from carolingian one was the lighter hink on manuscripts. This fact is reproduced by CSS through text opacity. The use of subscriptions reproduce the lines left by monks when they used special tools to avoid writing crooked. Paragraph lines are a bit more separated one from the other in order to represent the typical humanistic manuscript. In fact besides their inspiration to carolingian way of writing, humanistic writers prefers to use a lighter page with much more white space than past manuscripts. The humanistic way of writing born from Petrarca as a reaction to the heavy and dark page of late gothic manuscripts. Indeed it is why I tried to maintain a light page with a lot of space and a weaker color of text than usual. The initial sections' capital lettere is, on the other side, not philological, this fact is linked to the lack of historically correct capital letter fonts. After an unsuccesful research I used the most correct free font I was able to find on the internet.

This improvement started by the will of some people to feel immersed in a virtual space. During that period a lot of people tried to realise a sort of copy of the world, but soon this fashion died. People started to feel unconfort with a too loyal representation of the tangible reality. The development stopped between 2037 and 2045 due to the III world war between the Neo-URSS and the Great Capitalist America. After a tremendous war the Neo-URSS resulted victorius and smart contact lenses restarted to improve. The script

We created 2 different scripts for 2 different reasons. The first script is the one that change the content and the style of the iframe. a little Javascript is used firstly to change the SRC of the iframe and then through the onload wait until the page is loaded and then changing the CSS of the page. During the charge the iframe is hidden making impossible for users to see the changes going on.

The second script is used to automatically populate the page of the EU-directive. In fact at the beginning the page is empty and only through 2 fetch (a mechanism to handle in a simpler way calls to the server) the page is populated with all tags coming from 2 different HTML documents. We realized this script in order to have something that automatically take elements of different language versions of the same document and puts them in a grid without a long manual encoding.
